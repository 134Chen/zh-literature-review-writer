# Quality Checklist

Use this checklist before finalizing a literature review section.

## Search and Screening

- If the user asked to find papers, did you extract topic, object, task, target variable, and exclusion boundary first?
- Did you distinguish strong, medium, weak, and excluded candidates instead of treating all hits equally?
- Are kept papers truly tied to the topic, target task, or key contradiction rather than just sharing a method keyword?
- Did you verify basic bibliographic facts before using the paper?
- If a paper is only method-chain support, did you avoid presenting it as direct evidence on the exact topic?

## Topic Focus

- Is the text tightly centered on the user's topic rather than the whole field?
- If adjacent literature is introduced, does it directly serve the topic's method chain or unresolved problem?
- Is any paragraph drifting into control, policy, platform, system, or unrelated applications without user request?

## Paper-Level Writing

- Does each paper say what it did?
- Does each paper say what practical problem it solved?
- If "practical problem" is not the best framing, does it instead say what limitation remained and which later work improved it?
- Have you avoided reducing papers to "author + method name"?

## Claim Discipline

- Did you avoid unsupported claims such as "entered a new stage", "already mature", or "widely proved"?
- Are conclusions kept inside the actual scope of the cited work?
- Did you avoid exaggerated praise and empty academic filler?

## Style

- Is the prose compact and academic rather than slogan-heavy?
- Are transitions natural and restrained?
- Are concrete problems used more often than abstract praise words?

## Final Gap Statement

- Does the summary identify specific contradictions rather than a vague "existing methods are insufficient"?
- Does the final paragraph clearly explain why the user's chosen method is reasonable?
- If the user's method has multiple components, does the text map each component to a concrete unmet need?
